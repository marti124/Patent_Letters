Tribal Nation Letter and Brochure Generator

Version 1.0

Generate personalized cover letters, tribal procurement savings reports, and constitutional brochures for federally recognized tribal nations in the contiguous 48 states. Works for one tribe or hundreds.

Covers 346 federally recognized tribes across 33 states. Alaska Native entities (227) are excluded due to different governance structures.

Background: Why This Tool Was Created

This tool is a companion to the Sheriff Mailing Package Generator and the Governor/AG Letter Generator. Tribal nations are ideal constitutional plaintiffs: each is an independent sovereign government with a government-to-government relationship with the United States, no central authority can veto a tribe’s decision to file suit, and tribal governments have direct procurement injury from inflated vehicle and equipment prices.

The U.S. Constitution’s Patent Clause (Article I, Section 8, Clause 8) grants patent protection for “limited Times” — meaning that after a patent expires, the design enters the public domain for the public to use freely. But energy and safety regulations enacted after 1970 make it categorically impossible to remanufacture any vehicle or machine whose patents have expired. The designs never enter a functional public domain. This is costing American households an estimated $2,420 per year.

What This Tool Generates

From a state and tribe selection, the tool generates a 4-page mailing package:

  1. Cover Letter (1 page) — personalized with your contact info, the tribal leader’s name and office address, and tribe-specific Floor and Ceiling savings figures.
  2. Tribal Procurement Savings Report (1 page) — a detailed 6-category cost savings breakdown with Floor (15%) and Ceiling (45%) estimates.
  3. Constitutional Brochure (2 pages) — the full Patent Clause analysis adapted for tribal sovereignty, international evidence, standing theories, and call to action.

How to Use This Tool

  1. Open the HTML file by double-clicking it. It opens in your default browser. No account or internet connection required.
  2. Enter your personal information and click “Save My Info for Next Time.”
  3. Click the generate button to proceed to tribe selection.
  4. Select a state and tribe from the dropdowns — the leader’s name, title, and address auto-fill instantly from embedded data (updated March 2026). Fields remain editable.
  5. Verify if needed — click the “Verify with Google Search” link to confirm the name and address are current.

Important: When editing the leader’s name, enter just the name (e.g., “Chuck Hoskin Jr.” not “Principal Chief Chuck Hoskin Jr.”). The cover letter adds the title automatically from the Leader Title field.

Batch Mode

Generate packages for multiple tribes in one session:

  1. Select a state and tribe, look up the leader’s info.
  2. Click “Add to Batch” to queue it.
  3. Repeat for as many tribes as desired (you can mix states).
  4. Click “Preview & Print Entire Batch” — all tribes print in sequence, 4 pages each.

How the Tribal Procurement Savings Report Works

Every number is calculated from a single input: the tribe’s estimated on-reservation/service area population. All figures are modeled estimates using the same procurement-first methodology as the Sheriff v22 generator.

Step 1 — Estimate Annual Procurement

The model estimates what the tribal government currently spends annually buying new vehicles and equipment across six categories. Fleet sizes are population-based with adjustment factors; equipment categories use conservative per-capita procurement rates.

Step 2 — Apply Floor and Ceiling Discounts

Floor (15%): Total procurement × 15%. Savings if the tribe keeps buying only new equipment, but remanufactured competition forces prices down.

Ceiling (45%): Total procurement × 45%. Savings if the tribe purchases remanufactured equipment directly.
